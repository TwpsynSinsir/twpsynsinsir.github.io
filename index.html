Kia ora Taiao! (Hello World!)

Who am I?

My name is Calum Wicks. I'm 28 years old, and I currently live in Auckland, New Zealand. While I was also born here, I grew up entirely in Wales, over in the UK. It's a long way geographically, but Rugby and Sheep are still on the menu!

I grew up speaking English, however my schooling was entirely through the medium of Welsh, so I became fluent in both before I left for University. At University I studied Japanese, which I am also fluent in. (Just don't torment me with Keigo!)

My Homeland

Cymru, the land of song!

Despite not having been born in Wales, I grew up here and consider it my true homeland. I'm proud of Cymru's rich history in the arts, as the birthplace of the welfare state in Britain, and of the people, who through thick and thin throughout the ages have fought to keep their identity alive

Situated in the Western peninsula of Great Britain, Cymru is a land of valleys and mountains, rivers and ancient forests, and though they now lie silent, the mines deep within the earth used to hum with both song and great tragedy. Cymru and her people are survivors: We outlasted the Romans, the Saxons, the Normans, and stand as one of the oldest continuous lines of European nations.

The Japanese Connection

From one archipelago to another!

I began to study Japanese just before the end of Secondary School. Sadly, there were no Japanese classes I could take at school, and so I spent my time reading outdated textbooks from the local library and going to a night class a few towns over once a week.

I began studying Japanese formally at Oxford Brookes University. In my third year, I studied abroad in Japan at the University of Yamanashi. During this and my final year, I matured and gained a boatload of self-confidence. After University, I worked as an English teacher in Fukushima Prefecture primary schools for 2 years before coming to New Zealand to pursue further education.

Japan is a beautiful country, and I am blessed to have met many wonderful people in my time there. I hope to return to Japan this year alongside mypartner in a more long-term capacity.

Why am I studying at Dev Academy?

I've long suffered from an inability to choose a career path or decide what I wanted to be in the future. My initial job after graduation was teching English in Japan, but this was never meant to be a permanent career. After returning to New Zealand, I ping-ponged between jobs in construction, the university, and after-school tutoring, but nothing really stuck. Finally, after returning from a short study-abroad in Tokyo, I decided that I needed to settle on a career once and for all.

After much head-scratching and soul-searching, I decided to pursue programming. Previously, I had been interested in this sort of career, and had tried on a few half-hearted occasions to learn by myself, however they never really led anywhere. I happened upon Dev Academy by chance, and after chatting with a few of the teachers and getting a tour of the Auckland Campus, I signed on and have been enjoying it immensely so far!

Why Dev Academy in particular?

Nowadays, a career in the tech sector is a popular choice for many people, with the idea of being able to work remotely and gain competitive salaries compared to most other career paths. In response, many schools, bootcamps, online learning platforms and private individuals offer ways to learn different programming languages and 'kickstart a career in tech'. However, afer reading reviews and trawling through innumerable offers of 'quick hacks' and 'cheat codes' for learning programming skills, I decided to settle on Dev Academy Aoteroa. I made this decision because of a few factors:

  • Location: The Auckland Campus is practically a hop, skip and a jump down from my apartment.
  • Financial Support: Compared to other schools/bootcamps, I was able to take advantage of Study Link to lighten the load.
  • Career Support: Having access to Alumni and other advantages in job hunting are vital in the current job market.
  • Intuition: After talking with Rohan and Joseph, and visiting the Auckland campus, I felt confident that DAA was the right place!
