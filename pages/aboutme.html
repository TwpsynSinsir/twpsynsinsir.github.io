My Homeland

Cymru, the land of song!

Despite not having been born in Wales, I grew up here and consider it my true homeland. I'm proud of Cymru's rich history in the arts, as the birthplace of the welfare state in Britain, and of the people, who through thick and thin throughout the ages have fought to keep their identity alive

Situated in the Western peninsula of Great Britain, Cymru is a land of valleys and mountains, rivers and ancient forests, and though they now lie silent, the mines deep within the earth used to hum with both song and great tragedy. Cymru and her people are survivors: We outlasted the Romans, the Saxons, the Normans, and stand as one of the oldest continuous lines of European nations.


The Japanese Connection

An unlikely passion from one archipelago to another!
Image

I began to study Japanese just before the end of Secondary School. Sadly, there were no Japanese classes I could take at school, and so I spent my time reading outdated textbooks from the local library and going to a night class a few towns over once a week. I am forever indebted to my first tutor, Jac "Bara Caws", a lovely Japanese man who has sadly left us. He helped kindle the fire that I would carry on into tertiary education.

I began studying Japanese formally at Oxford Brookes University (no, not the fancy Oxford University), where in my third year, I studied abroad in Japan at the University of Yamanashi. During this year and my final year, I feel that I truly matured as a person and gained a boatload of self-confidence. After University, I worked as an English teacher in Fukushima Prefecture primary schools for 2 years before coming to New Zealand to pursue further education in Politics, International Relations, and then later Asian Studies again at a Postgraduate Level.

Japan is a beautiful country, and I am blessed to have met many wonderful people in my time there. I hope to return to Japan this year with my Japanese partner in a more long-term capacity.
